Forecasting Opportunity

Where Simulation and Machine Learning Help

Without prediction: Forecasting done based on what bound yesterday adjusted for operator knowledge. Real-time operators adjust with emergency reroutes, causing price spikes.
With a 24-hour forecast: Operators have the chance to pre-position resources and adjust schedules of generation in more ideal locations, deferring fast-ramp resource costs in real time prices.
Hypothesis: Flag high-risk hours and constraints. Operators mitigate emergency ramps and reroute to drive down cost.

Measurement

Not all mistakes are equal

The 96%/4% split creates a measurement problem. Standard accuracy metrics don't reflect real-world cost. Missing a $2M event hurts 20× more than a false alarm. The model needs to know this.
Business cost of each error type:

The principle: Standard forecasting optimizes for accuracy. We optimize for business cost, weighting errors by their financial impact on grid operations. The model is deliberately conservative on high-magnitude hours to avoid the costly tail.
Why Renewables Make This Harder

The evening ramp concentrates all the risk

Now we know which hours to catch and how much they cost. But when do these expensive hours occur? Solar variability creates a daily pattern.
Net load (after solar) Solar generation warning Ramp risk window
bolt The forecasting challenge:
Between 5–7pm, the grid needs to replace 3,000+ MW of lost solar while demand peaks. Any forecast error in solar or load forces power flows to shift fast.
Example: Forecast says 320 MW solar at 5pm. Actual: 272 MW (15% miss).
Gas plants in Zone A ramp harder to compensate. Line Z-14 hits capacity limits. Emergency redispatch costs $1.3M.
Why the model needs weather data: The ramp window is where small solar errors create binding events. Miss the clouds, miss the congestion.

Why separate encoders? Different signals matter on different days. On calm days, demand patterns dominate the forecast. During windstorms with outages, topology and weather become critical. Cross-attention shifts focus automatically.
Analogy: Four subject matter experts with one boss. The boss takes advice from the subject matter experts and decides how much each piece of advice matters this hour.
Benchmarking

Always beat persistence first

With the architecture defined, we need validation. But against what baseline? Raw accuracy is the wrong metric when 96% of hours are quiet.
Persistence forecasting ("tomorrow = today") is hard to beat. When your best model only improves by 2%, the data lacks signal and no architecture fixes that.
