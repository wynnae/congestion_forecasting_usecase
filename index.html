The Core Challenge

96% of hours are fine

96% of hours — small error, no risk 4% — large error, potential crisis
The trap: A model that always predicts "no risk" would be 96% accurate — and worthless.

Implications:

🎯 Precision over recall

Every alert triggers action. Tune for precision on high-cost events.

💡 Cost-weighted thresholds

$50K miss ≠ $2M miss. Alert threshold reflects expected cost.
Use the fraud-detection analogy: "This is the same problem as credit card fraud — you can't flag every transaction, but you can't miss the big ones. You optimize for precision in the high-cost tail, not overall accuracy." That framing resonates with any exec.
